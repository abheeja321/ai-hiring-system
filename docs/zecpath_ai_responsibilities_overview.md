# Zecpath AI Responsibilities Overview

Zecpath uses AI across the full hiring lifecycle to turn manual recruiting operations into a fast, explainable, and scalable talent pipeline. The first AI responsibility begins as soon as a recruiter creates a job post. Job Description Parsing AI converts raw hiring text into a structured job profile with role title, expected experience, required skills, preferred skills, education preferences, and responsibility clusters. This structured object becomes the source of truth for every downstream evaluation.

Once candidates submit resumes, Resume Parsing AI extracts clean text from PDF, DOCX, and text-based formats, normalizes headings and layout noise, detects sections such as skills, education, certifications, and work experience, and builds a structured candidate profile. Skill Extraction AI then identifies technical and non-technical skills, handles synonyms and grouped stacks, assigns confidence, and removes duplicate variants. Experience Parsing AI converts work history into comparable objects including company, title, dates, durations, and role relevance clues. Education and Certification AI standardizes academic history and professional credentials.

ATS Scoring AI uses the structured candidate and job profiles to produce an explainable shortlist score. It combines skill match, experience relevance, education alignment, and semantic similarity, then assigns the candidate to shortlist, review, or reject bands. This reduces recruiter workload while preserving transparency and auditability. Fairness and Normalization AI supports this stage by masking non-essential personal fields, reducing over-reliance on keyword stuffing, and standardizing evaluation logic across diverse resume formats.

After ATS shortlisting, Screening AI performs the first live interaction layer. In the Zecpath vision, this includes AI voice screening and transcript analysis to evaluate communication quality, intent alignment, and baseline role readiness. Qualified candidates move into Interview Intelligence, where HR Interview AI handles behavioral and policy-fit questioning, Technical Interview AI measures depth against role requirements, and Machine Test AI interprets practical assessment performance. Behavior Analysis AI works across these stages to identify collaboration patterns, confidence indicators, consistency, and fairness-sensitive signals without turning the system into a black box.

Final Decision AI acts as the orchestration layer that combines ATS output, screening performance, interview signals, machine test results, and behavioral analysis into one explainable recommendation. Its goal is not blind automation but decision support at scale: recruiters and hiring managers can review the score components, rationale, and confidence level before progressing to final approval. Once a candidate crosses the decision threshold, Offer Automation AI can prefill the final stage by generating status updates, offer-readiness flags, and backend triggers for approvals or document generation.

From a business standpoint, AI is required in Zecpath to achieve five core goals: recruiter automation, evaluation fairness, decision speed, operational scalability, and compliance readiness. Automation reduces repetitive manual review. Fairness controls improve consistency and reduce subjective bias. Speed helps teams move strong candidates before they drop out. Scalability allows thousands of applicants and concurrent interviews to be processed without linear HR growth. Compliance is supported through structured metadata, model versioning, audit trails, and explainable outputs at every stage. Together, these AI responsibilities define Zecpath as an end-to-end hiring intelligence platform rather than a single ATS feature.
